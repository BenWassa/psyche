# Self & Identity Domain — Layer 2 + 3

## Layer 2: Theories within Self & Identity

| Tier | Theory | Structural Type | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cornerstone** | Self-Efficacy (Bandura) | Dimensional / domain-specific | Massive applied evidence base; clear behavior-change mechanism |
| **Cornerstone** | Self-Compassion (Neff) | Dimensional / three-component | r ≈ .47 with wellbeing; replicates well across meta-analyses |
| **Include** | Self-Concept & Self-Schemas (Markus) | Cognitive / schema network | Useful organizing concept; not heavily tested as unitary theory |
| **Popular but revised** | Self-Esteem (global, comparative) | Construct critique | Not refuted, but increasingly seen as less useful than self-compassion |
| **Popular but revised** | Terror Management Theory | Process / motivational | Schimmack (2025) z-curve of 800+ studies suggests selection-bias-driven literature |

**Note on coherence:** This was the most unstable domain across research reports — the one most likely to feel thin or heterogeneous. The strategy: keep it tight (3 included theories), make cross-domain connections do heavy lifting, treat Self-Esteem and TMT as deliberate "popular but revised" entries that earn credibility.

**Note on what's *not* here:** Self-actualization lives in Motivation as part of the Maslow critique. Identity formation (Erikson) is deferred. Narrative identity (McAdams) has growing evidence but isn't at cornerstone-quality replication.

## Layer 3: Components within each theory

### Self-Efficacy (Bandura) — cornerstone

A *belief construct* that's domain-specific, not global.

```
Self-Efficacy
│
└── CORE DEFINITION
	└── Belief in one's capability to execute the actions
		required to manage prospective situations.
		*Domain-specific* — high in one area can coexist with low in another.
│
└── FOUR SOURCES (how self-efficacy is built or eroded)
	│
	├── 1. Mastery Experiences (most powerful)
	│   └── Past successes build belief; past failures erode it.
	│       Authentic, earned wins matter more than easy ones.
	│
	├── 2. Vicarious Experience
	│   └── Watching similar others succeed.
	│       "If they can, maybe I can."
	│       Model similarity matters.
	│
	├── 3. Verbal Persuasion
	│   └── Credible others' encouragement.
	│       Effect is real but bounded; can't substitute for mastery.
	│
	└── 4. Physiological & Emotional States
		└── How you read your body's signals.
			Reframing arousal as readiness vs threat shapes efficacy.
│
└── BEHAVIORAL CONSEQUENCES
	├── Goal choice (you choose harder goals when efficacy is high)
	├── Effort (you try harder)
	├── Persistence (you keep going through setbacks)
	└── Recovery (you bounce back from failure faster)

KEY INSIGHT NODE
└── Self-efficacy is built, not innate.
	Mastery > Watching > Being told > Reading your body.
	The most reliable way to raise self-efficacy is small wins
	in the actual domain you want to grow in.
```

**Total nodes:** 1 root + 4 sources + 4 consequences + 1 key insight = ~11 nodes.

**Visual grammar:** A *causal network* with directional arrows. Four inputs (sources) feeding one belief, which produces four behavioral outputs (consequences).

### Self-Compassion (Neff) — cornerstone

Three components in tension with their opposites.

```
Self-Compassion
│
└── THREE COMPONENTS (each with its opposite)
	│
	├── 1. Self-Kindness  ←→  Self-Judgment
	│   ├── Self-Kindness: warmth toward oneself in suffering or failure
	│   └── Self-Judgment: harsh internal criticism, shame
	│
	├── 2. Common Humanity  ←→  Isolation
	│   ├── Common Humanity: recognizing suffering as part of shared experience
	│   └── Isolation: feeling uniquely flawed, "only me"
	│
	└── 3. Mindfulness  ←→  Over-Identification
		├── Mindfulness: balanced awareness of painful thoughts/feelings
		└── Over-Identification: getting swept up in negative emotion,
			treating thoughts as facts
│
└── DISTINCT FROM SELF-ESTEEM
	├── Self-esteem: comparative, contingent on success
	├── Self-compassion: unconditional, doesn't require feeling above others
	└── Buffers against the failures self-esteem can't survive

CONSTRUCT VALIDITY NOTE
└── The two-factor structure of the SCS
	(positive vs. negative items) is debated.
	The construct works; the measurement has open questions.
```

**Total nodes:** 1 root + 3 component pairs (6 sub-nodes) + 1 self-esteem distinction + 1 measurement note = ~11 nodes.

**Visual grammar:** A **three-axis dimensional model** with each axis as a continuum between component and its opposite.

### Self-Concept & Self-Schemas (include — scaffolding)

A *cognitive structure* theory. Not as deeply tested, but useful connective tissue.

```
Self-Concept & Self-Schemas
│
└── CORE INSIGHT
	└── You hold cognitive structures about yourself —
		beliefs, traits, roles, narratives —
		that filter attention, memory, and behavior.
│
└── KEY DISTINCTIONS
	│
	├── Actual Self
	│   └── Who you think you are now
	│
	├── Ideal Self
	│   └── Who you want to be (hopes, aspirations)
	│
	└── Ought Self
		└── Who you feel you should be (duties, obligations)
│
└── DISCREPANCIES (Higgins' Self-Discrepancy Theory)
	├── Actual ≠ Ideal → dejection-related emotions (sadness, disappointment)
	└── Actual ≠ Ought → agitation-related emotions (anxiety, guilt)

KEY INSIGHT NODE
└── How you describe yourself shapes what you notice,
	what you remember, and how you behave.
	Self-concept isn't just a description — it's a filter.
```

**~8 nodes.** Functions as connective scaffolding — links to Personality (traits become self-schemas), to Cognition (schemas as System 1 outputs), to Emotion (discrepancies produce specific emotions).

### Self-Esteem (global) — popular but revised

```
Self-Esteem
│
└── Status: Useful concept, but increasingly superseded
	│
	├── What people know: feeling good about oneself; high self-esteem
	│   widely promoted as a goal in education, parenting, self-help
	│
	├── What survives:
	│   └── Domain-specific self-evaluations remain useful
	│       (e.g., academic self-concept, athletic self-concept)
	│
	├── What's wrong with the global construct:
	│   ├── Comparative ("I'm better than...") inflates narcissism
	│   ├── Contingent on success — collapses under failure
	│   ├── Self-esteem programs in schools showed weak/no effect
	│   │   on outcomes (Baumeister et al. 2003 review)
	│   └── Doesn't predict behavior change as cleanly as efficacy
	│
	└── What to use instead:
		Self-Efficacy (for capability beliefs)
		+ Self-Compassion (for self-relationship)
		give a more robust, less fragile framework.
```

**Single evidence card.** Re-contextualized, not refuted.

### Terror Management Theory — popular but revised

```
Terror Management Theory
│
└── Status: Largely compromised post-replication
	│
	├── What people know: humans manage existential terror of mortality
	│   by adopting cultural worldviews and pursuing self-esteem;
	│   reminders of death (mortality salience) intensify these defenses
	│
	├── What's wrong:
	│   ├── Schimmack (2025) z-curve analysis of 800+ studies:
	│   │   literature heavily biased by selective reporting
	│   ├── PLOS ONE multi-study failures (2023)
	│   ├── Schindler et al. (2023) meta-analysis with methodological controls
	│   │   shows much weaker effects than original 1.0+ d's
	│   └── Mortality salience effect appears largely artifactual
	│
	├── What survives:
	│   └── Existential concerns are real and shape behavior;
	│       the *specific* mortality-salience paradigm is fragile
	│
	└── What to use instead:
		Self-Concept (for identity coherence)
		+ Self-Compassion (for the existential reckoning)
		+ Adult Attachment (for the relational ground that buffers existential anxiety)
```

**Single evidence card.** Included specifically because TMT has cultural cachet.

## Cross-cutting tags applied

### Connections within Self & Identity (internal)

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Efficacy | Self-Concept | Beliefs about capability are part of the self-schema network |
| Self-Compassion | Self-Esteem critique | Self-compassion is what self-esteem programs were trying to deliver but failed at |
| Self-Concept — Ideal/Ought | Self-Compassion | Self-compassion buffers self-discrepancy emotions |

### Connections out of Self & Identity

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Efficacy | SDT — Competence (Motivation) | Conceptual siblings — same idea, two frameworks |
| Self-Efficacy | Goal-Setting (Motivation) | Self-efficacy is the primary mediator of goal pursuit |
| Self-Efficacy | Conscientiousness (Personality) | Trait basis of agentic action |
| Self-Compassion | Adult Attachment (Relationships) | Hill et al. (2025): secure attachment correlates with self-compassion |
| Self-Compassion | Emotion Regulation (Emotion) | Self-compassion supports adaptive ER, especially for failure/setback |

| Self-Concept — Discrepancies | Appraisal Theory (Emotion) | Self-discrepancies produce predictable emotional appraisals |
| Self-Concept | Cognitive Dissonance (Cognition) | Dissonance often involves self-concept threat |

- Include theory: Self-Concept & Self-Schemas (scaffolding role)
- Popular-but-revised: Self-Esteem and Terror Management Theory

## Design Notes for Implementation

- Self-Efficacy and SDT-Competence are conceptually identical — make this overlap explicit. They're not redundant; they're the same idea with different framing.
- Self-Compassion is the most connection-dense theory in the domain — it bridges to Attachment, Emotion, Personality, and Motivation. Consider making it a hub.
- Self-Concept does scaffolding work for the entire domain — it's where personality traits, cognitive schemas, emotional discrepancies, and self-beliefs meet.
- Self & Identity is the convergence point for threads from other domains. Teach this domain after others have established vocabulary if possible.
- Self-Esteem and TMT are both culturally prominent — including them with explicit correction is a credibility move.
