# Cognition Domain — Layer 2 + 3

## Layer 2: Theories within Cognition

| Tier | Theory | Structural Type | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cornerstone** | Dual Process Theory (Kahneman / Stanovich & Evans) | Process / framework | The organizing metaphor for fast vs slow thinking |
| **Cornerstone** | Heuristics & Biases (Tversky & Kahneman) | Process catalog | Anchoring, availability, base-rate neglect, framing remain robust |
| **Cornerstone** | Cognitive Behavioral Model (Beck) | Process / loop | Thought–emotion–behavior loop; bedrock of evidence-based practice |
| **Include** | Cognitive Dissonance (Festinger) | Process / motivational | Construct survives; classic 1959 paradigm did not replicate (Vaidis et al. 2024, 39-lab) |
| **Include (with caveat)** | Attribution Theory + Fundamental Attribution Error | Process / cognitive | Robust survival; magnitude varies cross-culturally; FAE less universal than canonically taught |

**Note on construct overlap:** Cognition is the most "interconnected" of the six domains — Dual Process, Heuristics, Dissonance, and Attribution all describe related machinery. The risk is presenting them as four disconnected theories when they're really four lenses on one cognitive system. Cross-references between them are critical.

## Layer 3: Components within each theory

### Dual Process Theory (cornerstone — but with caveats)

```
Dual Process Theory
│
└── TWO MODES OF THOUGHT
	├── System 1 (fast, automatic, intuitive)
	│   ├── Effortless, parallel
	│   ├── Pattern recognition, emotion
	│   ├── Operates by default
	│   └── Source of heuristics + biases
	│
	└── System 2 (slow, deliberate, analytical)
		├── Effortful, sequential
		├── Logic, deliberation, monitoring
		├── Engages when System 1 is challenged
		└── Limited capacity (cognitive load)

INTERACTION
├── System 1 generates → System 2 audits (sometimes)
├── Cognitive load shifts reliance toward System 1
└── Expertise can move tasks from System 2 → System 1

CAVEATS NODE (visible)
└── Status: Strong as framework, weaker as neuroscience
	├── Useful organizing metaphor — survives
	├── Not literally two brain systems
	├── Many specific demos in *Thinking Fast and Slow* failed replication
	└── Kahneman publicly acknowledged the priming chapter as the part he'd retract
```

**Total nodes:** 2 systems × ~4 properties + 3 interactions + caveat = ~12 nodes.

**Visual implication:** Two-pillar structure with bidirectional arrows between them. Not a hierarchy. Not a field. A *paired duality* — its own grammar variant.

### Heuristics & Biases (cornerstone — cataloged differently)

This isn't a hierarchical theory. It's a **catalog** of recurring patterns.

```
Heuristics & Biases
│
└── THE CORE SET (robustly replicated)
	│
	├── Anchoring
	│   └── First number you see disproportionately influences judgment
	│
	├── Availability
	│   └── Easily-recalled examples feel more frequent than they are
	│
	├── Representativeness
	│   └── Judging probability by resemblance to a stereotype, ignoring base rates
	│
	├── Base-rate neglect
	│   └── Underweighting prior probabilities in light of vivid evidence
	│
	├── Framing
	│   └── Identical information feels different depending on how it's presented
	│
	├── Confirmation bias
	│   └── Seeking, interpreting, recalling evidence that confirms existing beliefs
	│
	├── Loss aversion
	│   └── Losses feel ~2x more powerful than equivalent gains
	│
	├── Hindsight bias
	│   └── "I knew it all along" — past events feel more predictable in retrospect
	│
	└── (Each bias node includes: definition, example, debiasing tactic)

CAVEAT NODE
└── Status: Core biases robust; some specific 1970s demos fragile
	├── Anchoring, availability, framing remain strongly supported
	├── Some original Tversky-Kahneman demonstrations replicate weakly
	└── Teach as bounded tendencies, not universal irrationality
```

**Visual implication:** This wants to be a **card deck** or **catalog grid**, not a graph. Each bias is a self-contained card.

**Pruning note:** The full catalog has 100+ documented biases. The MVP set above is the ~8–10 with strongest empirical standing AND highest lay applicability.

### Cognitive Behavioral Model (cornerstone)

A *loop*, not a hierarchy.

```
CBT Model (Beck)
│
└── THE COGNITIVE TRIANGLE
	│
	├── Thoughts ←→ Feelings ←→ Behaviors
	│   (each shapes and reinforces the others, bidirectionally)
	│
	└── KEY OPERATIONS
		├── Automatic thoughts (System 1 outputs about the self/world/future)
		├── Cognitive distortions (predictable thinking errors)
		│   ├── Catastrophizing
		│   ├── All-or-nothing thinking
		│   ├── Mind-reading
		│   ├── Personalization
		│   └── Filtering (negative emphasis)
		├── Cognitive restructuring (audit + reframe distortions)
		└── Behavioral activation (action shifts thought/feeling)
```

**Total nodes:** 3 corners + 5 distortions + 3 operations = 11 nodes plus the loop itself.

**Visual implication:** A triangle with bidirectional arrows. The 5 distortions sit as cards under the "automatic thoughts" corner.

### Cognitive Dissonance (include, with paradigm caveat)

```
Cognitive Dissonance
│
└── THE CORE PHENOMENON
	├── Hold two conflicting cognitions → psychological discomfort
	└── Resolve via:
		├── Change a belief (rare)
		├── Change a behavior (sometimes)
		└── Rationalize ← System 1's preferred path
			├── Self-justification
			├── Selective attention to confirming info
			└── Effort justification ("if I worked hard, it must be worth it")

CAVEAT NODE
└── Status: Construct survives, classic paradigm did not
	├── Vaidis et al. (2024) 39-lab replication failed to support induced-compliance hypothesis
	├── The phenomenon (rationalization, self-justification) is everyday-observable
	└── Teach the construct, not the 1959 study
```

**5–8 nodes.** Functions as a *process loop* — tension → resolution path.

### Attribution Theory + FAE (include with cross-cultural caveat)

```
Attribution Theory
│
└── HOW WE EXPLAIN BEHAVIOR
	├── Internal (dispositional) — "they're like that"
	├── External (situational) — "the situation made them act that way"
	│
	└── COMMON ERRORS
		├── Fundamental Attribution Error
		│   └── Over-attribute others' behavior to disposition
		├── Self-Serving Bias
		│   └── My successes = me; my failures = circumstances
		├── Actor-Observer Asymmetry
		│   └── My behavior = situational; your behavior = dispositional
		└── In-group/Out-group Asymmetry
			└── In-group failures = situational; out-group failures = dispositional

CAVEAT NODE
└── Status: Robust survival; magnitude is culture-dependent
	├── FAE strongly demonstrated in Western samples
	├── East Asian samples show smaller effects
	└── Malle (2006) meta-analysis of actor-observer reduced effect substantially
```

**~9 nodes.** Direct overlap with Heuristics & Biases — FAE is itself a System 1 heuristic for person-perception.

## Cross-cutting tags applied

### Connections within Cognition (the dense interior)

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Dual Process — System 1 | Heuristics & Biases | System 1 generates the shortcuts |
| Dual Process — System 1 | Cognitive Distortions (CBT) | Same machinery; clinical framing of biased thought |
| Dual Process — System 2 | Cognitive Restructuring (CBT) | The intervention layer |
| Cognitive Dissonance | FAE | Dissonance often resolved via dispositional attribution to others |
| Heuristics & Biases | Attribution errors | FAE = a heuristic for explaining behavior |

### Connections out of Cognition

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| CBT loop | Appraisal Theory (Emotion) | Both argue: situations are interpreted, not directly experienced |
| CBT — Behavioral activation | Goal-Setting (Motivation) | Behavior changes thought/feelings |
| Heuristics & Biases | Social Identity (if added) | In-group bias as a social heuristic |
| FAE | Adult Attachment (Relationships) | Insecure attachment amplifies dispositional attribution toward partners |
| Dissonance | Self-Concept (Self & Identity) | Self-justification protects self-image |

### Evidence-weight tags
- Cornerstone theories: three anchors (Dual Process, Heuristics & Biases, CBT), all with full depth
- Include theories: full depth with caveats
- Popular-but-revised: none in this domain (all included theories survive with annotation)

### Structural-type tags
- Dual Process: paired-duality (two pillars with arrows)
- Heuristics & Biases: catalog/card-deck
- CBT: triangle/loop
- Dissonance: process loop
- Attribution: hierarchy with caveat

## Design Notes for Implementation

- Cognition needs four distinct visual grammars internally. Don't try to force them into one representation.
- The internal cross-references are critical — show these explicitly. Users should see that Heuristics & Biases are System 1 outputs, that CBT is applying System 2, that Attribution errors are heuristics.
- CBT's strength is its direct applicability — consider pairing each cognitive distortion with a reframing example.
- The Attribution Theory caveat about culture matters — consider regional/cultural examples where the effects differ.
- Dual Process can serve as the "entry point" theory that explains why the other three are necessary.
