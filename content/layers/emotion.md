# Emotion & Regulation Domain — Layer 2 + 3

## Layer 2: Theories within Emotion & Regulation

| Tier | Theory | Structural Type | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cornerstone** | Emotion Regulation Process Model (Gross) | Process / timeline | Dominant ER framework; reappraisal vs suppression distinction holds across meta-analyses |
| **Cornerstone** | Appraisal Theory (Lazarus) | Process / cognitive | Cognitive evaluations shape emotional response; foundation for reappraisal |
| **Include** | Constructed Emotion Theory (Lisa Feldman Barrett) | Process / theoretical perspective | Live theoretical debate; present as one perspective, not settled |
| **Popular but revised** | Broaden-and-Build Theory (Fredrickson) | Process / spiral | Broadening mechanism contested; positivity ratio mathematically refuted; core concept partially survives |

**Note on size and shape:** Two cornerstones, one debate-flagged inclusion, one popular-but-revised. No taxonomic theories here — Emotion is almost entirely process-based.

**Note on what's *not* here:** Basic Emotions Theory (Ekman's six universal emotions) gets significant pop coverage but has weak empirical standing. CBT model lives in Cognition with a connection bridge here.

## Layer 3: Components within each theory

### Gross's Process Model of Emotion Regulation (cornerstone)

This is a **timeline** theory. Five intervention points, ordered by where in the emotion process they intervene.

```
Process Model of Emotion Regulation
│
└── THE EMOTION TIMELINE (left → right)
	│
	Situation → Attention → Appraisal → Response
    
	Five intervention points along this timeline:
	│
	├── 1. Situation Selection (before emotion begins)
	│   └── Choose situations that lead to desired emotions
	│       (e.g., declining the dinner with a hostile colleague)
	│
	├── 2. Situation Modification (early in emotion)
	│   └── Change the situation to alter its emotional impact
	│       (e.g., asking a difficult conversation to be moved to private)
	│
	├── 3. Attentional Deployment (middle)
	│   └── Direct attention within the situation
	│       (e.g., focusing on what's working in a stressful meeting)
	│
	├── 4. Cognitive Change / Reappraisal (middle-late)
	│   └── Change how you interpret the situation
	│       (e.g., reframing nerves as excitement)
	│       *** the most empirically supported strategy ***
	│
	└── 5. Response Modulation (after emotion has emerged)
		├── Suppression (hiding expression) — least effective; backfires
		└── Adaptive expression, regulated breathing, exercise, etc.

KEY INSIGHT NODE
└── Earlier interventions (situation selection, modification, reappraisal)
	are systematically more effective than later ones (suppression).
	Suppression is associated with worse outcomes — physiological cost,
	relational cost, paradoxically more rumination.
```

**Total nodes:** 5 strategies + 1 key-insight node + brief examples per strategy = ~12 nodes plus the timeline itself.

**Visual grammar:** A horizontal **timeline** with the five strategies as marked entry points.

### Appraisal Theory (Lazarus) — cornerstone

Core mechanism behind reappraisal. Process flow.

```
Appraisal Theory
│
└── THE APPRAISAL CHAIN
	│
	Stimulus/Event
		↓
	Primary Appraisal: "Is this relevant to me? Good or bad?"
		├── Irrelevant → no emotion
		├── Benign / positive → positive emotion
		└── Stressful → continue to secondary appraisal
				↓
	Secondary Appraisal: "Can I cope with this?"
		├── Threat (potential harm, can't cope) → fear, anxiety
		├── Challenge (demanding but manageable) → eagerness, drive
		├── Harm/Loss (damage already done) → sadness, anger
		└── Benefit → relief, gratitude
				↓
	Emotion + Coping Response

CORE RELATIONAL THEMES (the appraisal patterns mapped to specific emotions)
├── Anger: demeaning offense against me/mine
├── Anxiety: facing uncertain, existential threat
├── Sadness: irrevocable loss
├── Guilt: having transgressed a moral imperative
├── Shame: failing to live up to an ego ideal
├── Pride: enhancement of identity by valued achievement
├── Gratitude: appreciating an altruistic gift
└── Compassion: being moved by another's suffering

KEY INSIGHT NODE
└── The same event produces different emotions in different people
	because they appraise it differently.
	This is what makes reappraisal possible —
	you can change the emotion by changing the interpretation.
```

**~14 nodes** (3 appraisal stages + 4 secondary appraisal outcomes + 8 core relational themes + key insight).

**Visual grammar:** A **flow diagram** with branching outcomes.

### Constructed Emotion Theory (include — flagged debate)

A *theoretical perspective* more than a structural theory.

```
Constructed Emotion Theory (Barrett)
│
└── CORE CLAIM
	└── Emotions are not universal, hardwired states.
		The brain constructs them from:
		├── Interoception (raw physiological data — heart rate, breath, gut)
		├── Past experience (what did this feel like before?)
		├── Cultural concepts (what do I have a name for?)
		└── Current context (what would fit this situation?)
│
└── IMPLICATIONS FOR THE USER
	├── Emotion granularity: more emotion words → finer-grained experience
	├── Emotional experience can be shaped over time
	├── Body awareness (interoception) shapes emotion
	└── "Anxious" and "excited" can be the same physiological state, differently constructed

DEBATE NODE (always visible)
└── Status: Influential but contested
	├── Active debate vs Basic Emotions Theory (Ekman)
	├── Meta-analyses fail to find unique neural fingerprints for specific emotions —
	│   supports constructionist account
	├── But theory is harder to falsify and remains theoretically debated
	└── Present as one perspective on emotion, not settled science
```

**~9 nodes.** Treated as a **perspective card** with implications, not a deep tree.

### Broaden-and-Build (Fredrickson) — popular but revised

```
Broaden-and-Build Theory
│
└── Status: Mechanism contested; positivity ratio refuted
	├── What people know: positive emotions broaden cognition
	│   and build psychological/social/physical resources over time
	│
	├── What survives:
	│   ├── Positive emotions correlate with wellbeing
	│   └── Some experimental support for cognitive broadening
	│
	├── What's wrong:
	│   ├── Positivity ratio (3:1) mathematically refuted
	│   │   (Brown, Sokal & Friedman 2013) — partially retracted
	│   ├── Roth et al. (2024) network analysis: broadening mechanism
	│   │   doesn't operate as hypothesized
	│   └── Build hypothesis replicates inconsistently
	│
	└── What to use instead:
		Appraisal Theory + Process Model + Self-Compassion give a
		more robust framework for cultivating wellbeing
```

**Single evidence card.** Exists to be searched and corrected.

## Cross-cutting tags applied

### Connections within Emotion (the dense interior)

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Appraisal Theory | Gross — Reappraisal | Appraisal is *what* gets changed in cognitive reappraisal |
| Appraisal Theory | Constructed Emotion | Both argue: emotion is built from interpretation, not directly inflicted |
| Gross — Suppression | Constructed Emotion | Suppression of the *expression* doesn't change the underlying construction |

### Connections out of Emotion

| From | To | Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| Appraisal Theory | CBT Model (Cognition) | Appraisal ≈ cognitive evaluation; CBT formalizes this for clinical use |
| Reappraisal | Dual Process — System 2 (Cognition) | Reappraisal requires deliberate cognitive engagement |
| Gross's timeline | Goal-Setting (Motivation) | Situation selection and modification are motivational acts |
| Suppression | Neuroticism (Personality) | High-N predicts greater reliance on suppression |
| Reappraisal | Conscientiousness (Personality) | High-C predicts greater use of reappraisal |
| Gross's framework | Adult Attachment (Relationships) | Attachment insecurity correlates with maladaptive ER strategies |
| Constructed Emotion — Granularity | Self-Compassion (Self & Identity) | Naming emotions precisely is part of mindful self-awareness |

### Evidence-weight tags
- Cornerstone theories: two anchors (Gross and Appraisal), both with full depth
- Include theory: Constructed Emotion with debate flag
- Popular-but-revised: Broaden-and-Build

### Structural-type tags
- Gross: horizontal timeline with entry points
- Appraisal: branching flow diagram
- Constructed Emotion: perspective card
- Broaden-and-Build: evidence card

## Design Notes for Implementation

- Emotion needs three distinct visual grammars: timeline (Gross), branching flow (Appraisal), and perspective card (Constructed Emotion).
- The most actionable theories are process theories — every theory here points to a practice or intervention. Consider surfacing practical guidance more prominently here than in other domains.
- Constructed Emotion's debate status is important pedagogically — it teaches users how science works when theories genuinely disagree.
- The Appraisal → Reappraisal connection is critical. Teach appraisal first so reappraisal makes sense.
- Gross's reappraisal vs suppression distinction is the strongest evidence point — make this prominent.
- Consider pairing this domain with Self-Compassion and Attachment — all three address "how I feel about myself and my relationships."
