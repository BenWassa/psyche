## node: self-compassion
title: Self-Compassion
tier: cornerstone
grammar: Field / Three axes

Neff's three-component model of a stable self-relationship: self-kindness, common humanity, and mindfulness — each a continuum against its opposite. Correlates with wellbeing around r ≈ .47 across meta-analyses.

### Structure
- Three axes, each in tension with an opposite: kindness ↔ judgment, common humanity ↔ isolation, mindfulness ↔ over-identification
- Unconditional, unlike self-esteem — it does not require success or feeling above others
- Buffers exactly the failures self-esteem cannot survive
- Not self-pity and not letting yourself off the hook — the evidence links it to more accountability, not less

### Note
The two-factor structure of the Self-Compassion Scale (positive vs negative items) is debated. The construct works; the measurement has open questions.

### Bridges
- [sc-kindness] Warmth toward yourself in failure.
- [sc-humanity] Suffering as shared, not isolating.
- [sc-mindfulness] Balanced awareness without drowning.
- [self-esteem] What self-esteem programs were reaching for, delivered without the fragility.
- [attachment] Secure attachment correlates with self-compassion; each can bootstrap the other.
- [emotion-regulation] Supports adaptive regulation, especially after failure and setback.

### Sources
- Neff (2003). Self-compassion: an alternative conceptualization of a healthy attitude toward oneself.
- Zessin, Dickhäuser & Garbade (2015). The relationship between self-compassion and well-being (meta-analysis).

## node: sc-kindness
title: Self-Kindness
tier: cornerstone
grammar: Axis / Kindness ↔ Judgment

Warmth toward yourself in suffering or failure, against the pull of harsh internal criticism.

### Structure
- The opposite pole is self-judgment: the internal critic, shame, contempt
- Operationally: speak to yourself the way you would to a struggling friend
- Not indulgence — kindness holds standards without cruelty

### Sources
- Neff (2003). Self-compassion: an alternative conceptualization.

## node: sc-humanity
title: Common Humanity
tier: cornerstone
grammar: Axis / Humanity ↔ Isolation

Recognizing suffering and failure as part of shared human experience, against the pull of "only me."

### Structure
- The opposite pole is isolation: feeling uniquely flawed
- Failure connects you to everyone who has ever failed — which is everyone
- The axis most directly opposed to shame

### Bridges
- [appraisal-themes] Shame's appraisal ("failing the ego ideal") is what this axis dissolves.

### Sources
- Neff (2003). Self-compassion: an alternative conceptualization.

## node: sc-mindfulness
title: Mindfulness
tier: cornerstone
grammar: Axis / Mindfulness ↔ Over-identification

Balanced awareness of painful thoughts and feelings, against the pull of being swept up in them.

### Structure
- The opposite pole is over-identification: treating thoughts as facts and moods as identity
- Sees the pain clearly without amplifying or suppressing it
- The precondition for the other two axes — you cannot be kind to what you will not look at

### Bridges
- [constructed-emotion] Precise emotional naming is mindfulness with better vocabulary.
- [cbt-automatic-thoughts] Noticing thoughts as thoughts is the shared move with CBT.

### Sources
- Neff (2003). Self-compassion: an alternative conceptualization.
